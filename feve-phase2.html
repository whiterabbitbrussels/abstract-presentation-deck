PART 1 · IDENTITY
BRUSSELSWHITERABBIT.EU
04

Positioning & identity.

VISUAL IDENTITY
  • Logo & icon system — all variants for digital and print: favicon, social avatars/banners, plus a simple logo animation.
  • Identity toolkit / brand guidelines — colour, typography, iconography and imagery.
  • Image style & art direction — moodboard and imagery direction tied to the messaging framework.
  • Template suite — PowerPoint master, Word letterhead, report / position-paper, minutes & agenda, Canva social, email signature.
  • Brand architecture — how the identity relates to Friends of Glass & Close the Glass Loop (endorsed, separate or realigned).
VERBAL & TEXTUAL IDENTITY
  • Tagline / descriptor — to carry the new name (e.g. "The Voice of European Glass Makers").
  • Messaging framework — value proposition and proof points, tied to FEVE's strategic priorities.
  • Tone of voice — practical guidance with examples.
  • Editorial style guide — spelling convention, glossary, hashtags, do/don't word list.
  • Boilerplate copy — short, medium and long descriptions for reuse across channels.
  • Launch roadmap — a one-page plan for sequencing the roll-out.

PART 2 · WEBSITE
BRUSSELSWHITERABBIT.EU
05

Website restructure & migration.

Simplify feve.org, sharpen the value proposition and apply the new identity — English-only, accessible, and live at launch.

STRUCTURE & EXPERIENCE
  • Information architecture & wireframes on a simplified, clearer structure.
  • UX & key user paths aligned to FEVE's stakeholders and priorities.
  • Design applying the new visual identity.
  • Content advisory — what stays, what goes, structure, length & format. FEVE owns and supplies all content.
TECHNICAL & MIGRATION
  • CMS implementation — WordPress (or comparable), editor-friendly.
  • Content migration from the current site.
  • New domain set-up — 301 redirects & SEO carry-over from feve.org.
  • Accessibility to WCAG 2.1 AA, plus basic SEO.
  • Launch support — site live at the brand launch. Optional hosting & maintenance from 2027, quoted separately.
